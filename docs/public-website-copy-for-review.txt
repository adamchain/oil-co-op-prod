HOMEPAGE — ABOUT (#about)
================================================================================

Section heading: Oil Co-op members pay less

Section intro:
  Citizen's Oil Co-op is a buyers' club: strength in numbers for lower prices
  and someone in your corner if something goes wrong.

Body paragraph 1:
  Through group purchasing, many members save on the order of $250–$300 per
  heating season. The Co-op has published examples of roughly $500 per heating
  season vs. Connecticut state averages for full-service oil at ~900 gallons.
  Pricing is often roughly 40–60¢ per gallon below average posted prices, with
  total annual savings depending on usage and market conditions.

Body paragraph 2:
  You join the Co-op, we identify a participating full-service company that
  fits your town and needs, and that company contacts you to set up service at
  the Co-op's negotiated rate. It's that easy.

Body paragraph 3:
  Membership is not a lock-in contract for a fixed price — you keep control of
  your service once you're set up with a supplier. Variable pricing has helped
  many families save in recent markets.

HOMEPAGE — OUR STORY (#story)
================================================================================

Section heading: Our story

Paragraph 1:
  The Co-op began in 1981 in connection with a non-profit; when that
  organization wound down, the founders continued the mission of fair pricing
  for Connecticut residents. Citizen's Oil Co-op was incorporated in June 1992
  to unite heating oil consumers and leverage purchasing power.

Paragraph 2:
  Family-owned and operated, the Co-op has grown to over 3,000 members across
  every town in Connecticut and Rhode Island, with expansion into parts of New
  York and Massachusetts (including much of Worcester, Norfolk, and Bristol
  counties in MA and Westchester County, NY).

Paragraph 3:
  Today the Co-op also negotiates programs beyond oil — propane, bioheat, solar,
  audits, insurance, and more — with the same focus: affordable, quality
  full-service energy and advocacy for members.

HOMEPAGE — MEMBERSHIP (#membership)
================================================================================

Section heading: Membership
Section intro: Fees, delivery, referrals, and what membership does not mean.

--- Costs & fees ---

  • $10 non-refundable application fee.
  • $35 annual membership dues, renewing each year.
  • $25 annual rate for seniors 55+ (also applies to some low-volume propane
    accounts per Co-op policy).

Online signup note:
  If you join through this app, charged amounts may follow your configured
  payment system — confirm current published fees with the office.

--- Delivery ---

  Most participating companies use automatic delivery. Some areas may allow
  will-call (you schedule with a few days' notice) — ask the Co-op what's
  available where you live.

  If you leave the Co-op, you must end delivery arrangements with your oil or
  propane company directly; the Co-op cannot cancel for you. You must also
  cancel membership with the Co-op to stop renewals.

--- Referral program ---

  Refer five new active members and become a lifetime member with no annual
  dues. Individual referrals may qualify for waived dues in an upcoming season
  or promotional raffles (e.g. gift cards) when announced.

--- The Next Step program ---

  Nonprofits and community groups can introduce the Co-op to their members. For
  each person who joins, the Co-op donates $10 back to that organization (paid
  bi-annually when totals exceed $50 per period, otherwise carried forward).
  Next Step and the member referral program cannot be combined on the same
  signup.

Sidebar callout — We're on your side:
  Large membership means better contracts — and a voice when you need help with
  pricing, service contracts, or supplier issues.

Pills/tags:
  Not a fixed-price lock-in
  Full-service suppliers
  Advocacy

Section heading: Services

--- Heating oil (accordion) ---

  All Co-op heating oil suppliers are full-service. Members typically see
  pricing roughly 40–50¢ below average state posted prices, with competitive
  service contracts and access to budget billing programs (often arranged
  before September 1).

  You're billed by your company with time to pay — and the Co-op can help
  advocate if you have service or pricing questions.

--- Propane (accordion) ---

  Discounted propane pricing; many members receive added benefits such as free
  tank rental. The Co-op forwards your information to a participating supplier,
  who contacts you, performs a safety check, switches equipment as needed, and
  coordinates removal of the old tank.

  Existing Co-op members can often add propane without a second membership fee.
  Many oil suppliers also deliver propane for stacked savings.

--- Bioheat (B20) (accordion) ---

  B20 is 80% low-sulfur No. 2 oil and 20% biodiesel — usable in existing oil
  equipment. Biodiesel in the blend is sourced to meet ASTM standards (e.g.
  Greenleaf Biofuels).

  Environmental highlights often cited for B20 include:
  • Meaningful reductions in CO₂, particulates, and other emissions vs.
    conventional oil.
  • Supports domestic fuel production and reduced reliance on imported oil.

  Bioheat is available in many Connecticut towns; where it isn't, rallying
  interest (e.g. ~10 neighbors) can help the Co-op open a route.

--- Solar energy (accordion) ---

  Solar can reduce purchased electricity with a fixed energy rate. Many
  households save a significant share vs. utility supply costs; federal (and
  sometimes state) incentives apply — ask for current programs.

  A Co-op representative can review your home and usage. Members who enroll
  through the Co-op have at times qualified for a $500 incentive upon project
  completion and activation (verify current offer).

--- Home energy audits (NESE) (accordion) ---

  Partner New England Smart Energy (NESE) offers audits across Connecticut with
  a modest copay (often around $50), including substantial in-home measures
  (historically up to ~$600 of work) and access to rebates on follow-up
  improvements.

  Pairing audits with cleaner fuels like bioheat can cut both bills and carbon
  footprint.

--- Insurance (Bearingstar) (accordion) ---

  Partner Bearingstar Insurance offers member pricing on auto and homeowners
  coverage. Testimonials cite hundreds of dollars in annual savings vs. prior
  carriers.

--- Electricity (accordion) ---

  Status: The Co-op has previously offered electricity programs; as of recent
  updates, there may not be a live electric supply offer while a new supplier
  relationship is pursued. Call or email for the latest.

--- Blue Earth Compost & more (accordion) ---

  The Co-op highlights additional member programs on oilco-op.com — including
  organics/compost and other seasonal offers. This site focuses on core energy
  programs; visit the main site for the full menu.
